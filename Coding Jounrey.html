I got into coding at a young age of 16 after I stopped playing high school sports. I never really enjoyed playing , its just something that I got into when I was young and never stopped. However, after quitting I found myself with a lot of free time and decided to take up coding. Unfortunately, it was an onn and off relationship. I would learn how to code in Python for about 2 months, then stop for a while, just to try to pick it up again, rinse and repeat. This prevented me from actually truly learning how to code. This translated over to my college years, where instead of following my for coding, I went into biology to become a dentist. Fortunately, I had more time to think this through during the COVID-19 lockdowns and decided to fully commit to my passion by enrolling in this program.

One of the reasons I decided to pursue a career in coding is the fact that I love playing video games. I owned almost every console and about 6 years ago, brought a PC for gaming. This PC allowed me to get into the coding aspect of gaming. After a while, I started to explore game development. I learned Unity and Unreal Engine, C# and C++, as these are the languages and frameworks most games are written in. While I have a long way to go, I am proud of the progress I made so far. Currently, I am in the very early stages of a Football game. I plan to include and learn more about collision detection, realistic movement and animation in Unity. Hopefully, in the near future, I have enough knowledge and skill to make my own game engine and no longer have to rely on Unity and Unreal.
